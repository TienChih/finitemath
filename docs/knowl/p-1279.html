Recall that \(n=12, p=0.3.\) Let \(X\) denote the number of team members have a conflict.

  1. This is \(P(X=3)=C(12,6)(0.3)^6(0.7)^6\approx0.0792\text{.}\)
  2. This is the sum of three through twelves members having a conflict, but its also just removing cases zero through three having a conflict. So
    \begin{align*} \amp1-P(X=0)-P(X=1)-P(X=2)-P(X=3)\\ \amp=1-C(12,0)(0.3)^0(0.7)^{12}-C(12,1)(0.3)^1(0.7)^{11}-C(12,2)(0.3)^2(0.7)^{10}-C(12,3)(0.3)^3(0.7)^{9}\\ \amp \approx 1-0.4925=0.5075. \end{align*}
  3. This is \(E(X)=np=12(0.3)=3.6\) team members.

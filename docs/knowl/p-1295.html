There are two ways to look at this problem. We could choose to select 0 of the 5 steps to be “rights”, or 1, 2, 3, 4 and up to 5 of them to be “rights”. The correspond to to walks ending in \((0,5), (1,4), (2,3), (3,2), (4,1), (5,0)\text{.}\) These are all the possible endpoints of a length 5 walk. So by summing the number of all such walks together, we can enumerate all length 5 walks. This gives us

\begin{equation*} {5 \choose 0}+{5 \choose 1}+{5 \choose 2}+{5 \choose 3}+{5 \choose 4}+{5 \choose 5}=1+5+10+10+5+1=32 \end{equation*}

Remark 5.1.3.

Let \(P\) denote the present value of a investment/debt growing via simple interest. Let \(A\) denote the future value, \(r\) denote the simple interest rate per time period and \(m\) the number of time periods. Then

\begin{equation*} A=P(1+rm). \end{equation*}

Be sure you keep track of what time period (years, days, months etc.) you're using and stay consistent.

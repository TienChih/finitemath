Since it is a even degree polynomial with positive leading coefficient, it goes up to \(\infty\) as \(x\) goes to both positive and negative \(\infty\text{.}\) We also see that \(p(x)=0\) whenever \(x=-1\) or \(x=1\text{.}\) The multiplicity of both of these roots is 2, so \(p(x)\) “bounces” at both \(x=1\) and \(x=-1\text{.}\)

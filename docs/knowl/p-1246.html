Recall the probability distribution of poisened apples.

\begin{equation*} \begin{array}{|c|cccc|} \hline x \amp 0 \amp 1 \amp 2 \amp 3 \\ \hline P(X=x) \amp \frac{35}{210}\approx 0.1667\amp \frac{105}{210}=0.5 \amp \frac{63}{210}=0.3 \amp \frac{7}{210}\approx 0.0333\\ \hline \end{array} \end{equation*}

So the expected value of poisened apples would be

\begin{equation*} E(X)=0\cdot\frac{35}{210}+1\cdot\frac{=105}{210}+2\cdot\frac{63}{210}+3\cdot\frac{7}{210}=1.2. \end{equation*}

Solution 6.5.3.1.

We first find the growth factor \(a\text{:}\)

\begin{align*} 4=d(1)\amp = ba^1\\ 2=d(4)\amp = ba^4\\ \frac{4}{2}\amp = \frac{ba^1}{ba^4}\\ 2\amp = \frac{1}{a^3}\\ a^3\amp = \frac{1}{2}\\ a\amp = (\frac{1}{2})^{1/3}=\frac{1}{2^{1/3}}=2^{-1/3}\approx 0.7937 \end{align*}

From here we may find \(b\text{:}\)

\begin{align*} 4=d(1)\amp = b(2^{-1/3})^1\\ b\amp=4\cdot 2^{1/3}=2^{7/3}\approx 5.0396842 \end{align*}

Thus \(d(t)=2^{7/3}\cdot (2^{-1/3})^t\text{.}\)

Item 2.
Note that since we are picking both 2 Mathematicians and 2 Historians, not one or the other, that the Multiplication Principle, not the Addition Principle applies, so \(C(8,2)\cdot C(12,2)=28\cdot 66=1848\text{.}\) We can think of this as 28 ways of picking Mathematicians, 66 ways of picking historians, and 1848 ways of putting the Math and History pairs together.

Definition 9.4.6.

Given a finite random variable \(X\text{,}\) it's expected value is the predicted average outcome of experiments, and is computed:

\begin{equation*} E(X)=\sum P(X=x)\cdot x. \end{equation*}

We think of this as the outcomes of \(X\text{,}\) weighted by their likelihood, so the more likely outcomes contribute more than the less likely ones.

Note that the “Expected Value” may not be a value we actually expect, that is, may not be one of the outcomes, just an average outcome.

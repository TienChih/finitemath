Remark 10.4.1.

Given a binomial random variable \(X\) with parameters \(n\) trials, each with probability of success \(p\text{,}\) then we may approximate \(X\) with \(X'\text{,}\) a normal variable with mean \(\mu=np\) and standard deviation \(\sigma=\sqrt{np(1-p)}\text{,}\) so long as:

  • \(np\geq 10\text{.}\)
  • \(n(1-p)\geq 10\text{.}\)

The greater \(n\) is, the better the approximation will be.

Solution 2.4.3.1.

Consider the matrix \(A\) which represents a transformation from Products to Ingredients:

\begin{equation*} A=\begin{pmatrix} 2\amp 3 \amp 0 \\ 2\amp 0 \amp 3 \\ 0\amp 1 \amp 1\end{pmatrix}. \end{equation*}

We can compute the inverse matrix from Ingredients to Products:

\begin{equation*} A^{-1}=\begin{pmatrix} \frac{1}{4}\amp \frac{1}{4} \amp -\frac{3}{4} \\ \frac{1}{6}\amp -\frac{1}{6} \amp \frac{1}{2} \\ -\frac{1}{6}\amp \frac{1}{6} \amp \frac{1}{2}\end{pmatrix}. \end{equation*}

So if we had 400 quarts of pineapple juice, 400 quarts of orange juice and 100 quarts of kiwi juice, the amount of product we could make would be:

\begin{equation*} \begin{pmatrix} \frac{1}{4}\amp \frac{1}{4} \amp -\frac{3}{4} \\ \frac{1}{6}\amp -\frac{1}{6} \amp \frac{1}{2} \\ -\frac{1}{6}\amp \frac{1}{6} \amp \frac{1}{2}\end{pmatrix} \begin{pmatrix} 400\\400\\100 \end{pmatrix}= \begin{pmatrix}125 \\ 50\\50\end{pmatrix} \end{equation*}

That is, 125 gallons of PineOrange, 50 gallons of PineKiwi and 50 gallons of OrangeKiwi.

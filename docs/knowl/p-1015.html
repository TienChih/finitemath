We can use either the Desmos calculator above, or algebra:

  • The average rate of change of \(f(x)\) over the interval \([0,2]\) is
    \begin{align*} \frac{f(2)-f(0)}{2-0}\amp = \frac{-5(2^2)+20(2)-(-5(0^2)+20(0))}{2}\\ \amp = \frac{20-0}{2}\\ \amp = 10. \end{align*}
    So the average rate of change in the height of the rocket between 0 and 2 seconds is 10 m/s.
  • The average rate of change of \(f(x)\) over the interval \([0,1]\) is
    \begin{align*} \frac{f(1)-f(0)}{2-0}\amp = \frac{-5(1^2)+20(1)-(-5(0^2)+20(0))}{1}\\ \amp = \frac{15-0}{1}\\ \amp = 15. \end{align*}
    So the average rate of change in the height of the rocket between 0 and 1 seconds is 15 m/s.
  • The average rate of change of \(f(x)\) over the interval \([0,0.5]\) is
    \begin{align*} \frac{f(0.5)-f(0)}{0.5-0}\amp = \frac{-5(0.5^2)+20(0.5)-(-5(0^2)+20(0))}{0.5}\\ \amp = \frac{8.75-0}{0.5}\\ \amp = 17.5. \end{align*}
    So the average rate of change in the height of the rocket between 0 and 0.5 seconds is 17.5 m/s.
  • The average rate of change of \(f(x)\) over the interval \([0,2]\) is
    \begin{align*} \frac{f(2)-f(1)}{2-1}\amp = \frac{-5(2^2)+20(2)-(-5(1^2)+20(1))}{1}\\ \amp = \frac{20-15}{1}\\ \amp = 5. \end{align*}
    So the average rate of change in the height of the rocket between 1 and 2 seconds is 5 m/s.
  • The average rate of change of \(f(x)\) over the interval \([1,1.25]\) is
    \begin{align*} \frac{f(1.25)-f(1)}{1.25-1}\amp = \frac{-5(1.25^2)+20(1.25)-(-5(1^2)+20(1))}{0.25}\\ \amp = \frac{17.1875-15}{0.25}\\ \amp = 8.75. \end{align*}
    So the average rate of change in the height of the rocket between 1 and 1.25 seconds is 8.75 m/s.
  • The average rate of change of \(f(x)\) over the interval \([1,1+h]\) is
    \begin{align*} \frac{f(1+h)-f(1)}{1+h-1}\amp = \frac{-5((1+h)^2)+20(1+h)-(-5(1^2)+20(1))}{h}\\ \amp = \frac{-5(h^2+2h+1)+20(1+h)-15}{h}\\ \amp = \frac{-5h^2-10h-5+20+20h-15}{h}\\ \amp = \frac{-5h^2+10h}{h}=\frac{(-5h+10)h}{h}\\ \amp = -5h+10. \end{align*}
    So the average rate of change in the height of the rocket between 1 and \(1+h\) seconds is \(-5h+10\) m/s.
  • The average rate of change of \(f(x)\) over the interval \([a,a+h]\) is
    \begin{align*} \frac{f(a+h)-f(a)}{a+h-a}\amp = \frac{-5((a+h)^2)+20(a+h)-(-5(a^2)+20(a))}{h}\\ \amp = \frac{-5(a^2+2ah+h^2)+20(a+h)-(-5a^2+20a)}{h}\\ \amp = \frac{-5a^2-10ah-5h^2+20a+20h+5a^2-20a}{h}\\ \amp = \frac{-10ah-5h^2+20h}{h}=\frac{(-10a-5h+20)h}{h}\\ \amp = -10a-5h+20. \end{align*}
    So the average rate of change in the height of the rocket between \(a\) and \(a+h\) seconds is \(-10a-5h+20\) m/s.

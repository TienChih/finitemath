In general, \(P(A)=\frac{3}{6}\text{,}\) however, if we know \(B\) is true, there are no longer 6 valid outcomes. We are restricted to \(B=\{4,5,6\}\) as our new sample space. So we note that out of these, \(\{4,6\}\) are are even. So the probability now that \(A\) occurs is \(\frac{2}{3}\text{.}\)

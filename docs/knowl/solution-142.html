Solution 9.2.8.1.

We note that there are 11 non-poisened apples.

  1. Ths is picking 4 of 15 objects so \(C(15,4)=1365\text{.}\)
  2. Ths is picking 4 of 11 objects so \(C(11,4)=330\text{.}\)
  3. Ths is picking 4 of 5 objects so \(C(5,4)=5\text{.}\)
  4. Ths is picking 2 of 5 objects to be posiened, 2 of 11 objects for not poisened, and combining these selections, so \(C(5,2)\cdot C(11,2)=10\cdot55 =550.\text{.}\)
  5. This is either no apples poisened, which has 330 possibilies as seen above, or 1 apple poisened, counted \(C(5,1)\cdot C(11,3)=5\cdot 165=825\text{.}\) Thus we have \(330+825=1155\) ways to have no or one poisened apple.

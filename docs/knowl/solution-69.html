Solution 6.2.2.1.

We note that the monthly revenue generated by the apartment complex is \(O\cdot T\) where \(O\) is the occupancy and \(T\) is the rent (the rent per unit, times the number of units rented).

We note that if \(x\) is the amount in dollars above \(\$800\) that you charge, then \(O=80-\frac{x}{25}\text{,}\) that is, occupancy starts at 80 and decreases one per \(\$25\text{.}\) On the other hand, the rent \(T=800+x\text{.}\) So \(R(x)=(80-\frac{x}{25})(800+x)=-\frac{1}{25}x^2+48x+64000\text{.}\)

So our vertex occurs at \(h=-\frac{48}{2\cdot \frac{-1}{25}}=600\) and \(k=64000-\frac{48^2}{4\cdot \frac{-1}{25}}=78400\text{.}\)

Since this \(h=600\) is how much the rent is increased, the revenue is maximized when the rent is \(800+600=\$1400\) a month, with an occupancy of \(80-\frac{600}{25}=56\) units, for a total revenue of \(\$78400\text{.}\)

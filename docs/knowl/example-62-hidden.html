Let

\begin{equation*} p(x)=x^3-3x^2=x^2(x-3)\text{.} \end{equation*}

Since it is a odd degree polynomial with positive leading coefficient, it goes up to \(\infty\) as \(x\) goes to \(\infty\) and down to \(-\infty\) as \(x\) goes to \(-\infty\text{.}\) We also see that \(p(x)=0\) whenever \(x=0\) or \(x=3\text{.}\) The multiplicity of 0 is 2, and the multiplicity of 3 is 1, which are even and odd respectively, so \(p(x)\) “bounces” at \(x=0\) and crosses at \(x=1\text{.}\)

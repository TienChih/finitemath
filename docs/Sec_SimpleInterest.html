Section 5.1 Simple Interest

In this section, we discuss simple interest, a way of computing interest based solely on the principle.

Definition 5.1.1.
Given a initial investment or loan of \(\$P\text{,}\) often called a principle or present value of an investment/loan, then the simple interest of that principle at a rate of \(r\) per time unit, over \(n\) time units will be
\begin{equation*} I=Prn. \end{equation*}

Example 5.1.2.
  1. If you invest \(\$500\) at a rate of \(1\%\) simple interest a month, then in 18 months, you will have accumulated \(i=500\cdot 0.01\cdot18=\$90\) of interest.
  2. If you put \(\$1000\) at a rate of \(8\%\) annual simple interest, then in 6 years, you will have accumulated \(i=1000\cdot 0.08\cdot6=\$240\) of interest.
  3. If you borrow \(\$5000\) from a loan shark at a rate of \(2\%\) simple interest a day, then in a week, you will have accumulated \(i=5000\cdot 0.02\cdot7=\$700\) of interest.

Assuming no payments or additional principles are taken out, this interest is accumalated in addition to the original principle. If we let \(A\) denote the accumalated future value:

\begin{equation*} A=P+It \end{equation*}

at time \(t\text{.}\)

Let's say you take out a loan at $100 dollars and are charged 5% monthly simple interest. What this means is that every month, 5% of your initial debt is added on as interest. Over time you would see something like:

\begin{equation*} \begin{array}{|c|c|} \hline \text{ Month } \amp \text{ Debt } \\ \hline 0 \amp 100\\ 1\amp 100+(.05)(100)(1)=100+5=105\\ 2\amp 100+(.05)(100)(2)=100+10=110\\ 3 \amp 100+(.05)(100(3)=100+15=115\\ \vdots \amp \vdots\\ n \amp 100+(.05)(100)n=100+5n\\ \hline \end{array} \end{equation*}

So in say 5 months you would owe \(100+5\cdot 5=\$125\text{.}\) In general, we could track how much we owe \(A\) in month \(m\) as \(A=100+5m\text{,}\) which is, as we know, a linear equation:

Now of course, this could be adjusted to any number of situations. You could take out a loan for \(P\) dollars. You could have a \(r\) interest rate every time period which doesn't have to be a month. Then the future value \(A\) after \(m\) time periods would be:

\begin{equation*} \begin{array}{|c|c|} \hline \text{ Time Unit } \amp \text{ Debt } \\ \hline 0 \amp P\\ 1\amp P+rP\\ 2\amp P+rP+rP=P+2rP\\ 3 \amp P+2rP+rP=P+3rp\\ \vdots \amp \vdots\\ m \amp P+mrP=P(1+mr)\\ \hline \end{array} \end{equation*}

This still expresses \(A\) as a line with \(y\)-intercept \(P\) and slope \(rP\text{:}\)

So suppose you lend your friend some money at 2% weekly interest, and after 10 weeks they owe you $240, how much did you lend them? Well, we know that in this case \(r=0.02, w=10\) and the future value \(A=240\) so:

\begin{align*} A\amp = P+Prn\\ 240\amp = P+P(10)(.02)\\ 240\amp = 1.2P\\ P\amp = 200 \end{align*}

So \(P=\$200\) is how much they borrowed. One can verify this graphically:

Checkpoint 5.1.3.

If I invest \(\$1000 \) at 12% simple annual interest, how long until my investment is worth \(\$2000\text{?}\)

Solution

We're given \(P=1000, A=2000, r=0.12\text{.}\) Thus we have:

\begin{align*} A\amp = P+Prn\\ 2000\amp = 1000+(1000)(.12)n\\ 1000\amp = 120n\\ n\amp = \frac{1000}{120}\approx 8.333\ldots \end{align*}

So in 8 years and four months:

Checkpoint 5.1.4.

If I borrow \(\$750 \) and in 5 years, I owe \(\$1000\text{,}\) how much was the annual simple interest rate?

Solution

We're given \(P=750, A=1000, n=5\text{.}\) Thus we have:

\begin{align*} A\amp = P+Prn\\ 1000\amp = 750+(750)r(5)\\ 250\amp = 3750r\\ r\amp = \frac{250}{3750}\approx 0.066\ldots \end{align*}

So about 6.67\% annual simple interest:

Checkpoint 5.1.5.

How much should I invest so that in 20 years, I have \(\$10,000\) at an 8% annual simple interest rate?

Solution

We're given \(A=10000, n=20, r=0.08\text{.}\) Thus we have:

\begin{align*} A\amp = P+Prn\\ 10000\amp = P+P(0.08)(20)\\ 10000\amp = P(1+1.6)\\ P\amp = \frac{10000}{2.6}\approx 3846.15\ldots \end{align*}

So about \(\$3846.15\) in inital investment:

Checkpoint 5.1.6.

How much should will I owe in 15 weeks, if I borrow \(\$5,000\) at an 2% weekly simple interest rate?

Solution

We're given \(P=5000, n=15, r=0.02\text{.}\) Thus we have:

\begin{align*} A\amp = P+Prn\\ A\amp = 5000+5000(0.02)(15)= 6500.00 \end{align*}

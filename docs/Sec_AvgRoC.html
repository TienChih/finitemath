Section 7.2 Average rates of change

Linear functions have a coinstant rate of change, the slope. But as we have seen, other functions can increase, or decrease, at varying rates. Can we, looking at an interval of time, determine the average rate of change of a function?

Subsection 7.2.1 Average Rate of Change as a Slope

Consider a rocket that is launched into the air and has height \(f(x)=-5x^2+20x\) meters in \(x\) seconds. How fast is the rocket traveling when it's launched (\(x=0\) seconds)?

Turns out, it's not so easy to think about what this is. We don't typically consider speeds in terms of instantaneous action. Our methods of measuring speed reflects this, units of speed or velocity take the form of meters per second, miles per hour, in other words speed is some change in distance per time unit and that unit of time is not typically zero.

So what now? Lets start by asking an easier question, what is the average speed of the rocket over the first 3 seconds?

It's best in a scenario like this to not overthink the underlying problem. At 0 seconds, the rocket is \(f(0)=-5(0)^2+20(0)=0\) meters off the ground. After 3 seconds it is now \(f(3)=-5(3^2)+20(3)=15\) meters off the ground. So if the rocket rose by 15 meters in 3 seconds? You're average speed must be 5 meters per second.

Definition 7.2.1.

The average rate of change of a function \(f(x)\) over the interval \([a,b]\) is:

\begin{equation*} m=\frac{f(b)-f(a)}{b-a} \end{equation*}

This is just the slope of the line connecting \((a,f(a))\) and \((b,f(b))\text{.}\)

Example 7.2.2.

In our rocket example above, where the height is \(f(x)=-5x^2+20x\) meters in \(x\) seconds, find the rate of change of the rocket over the following periods of time:

  • The interval \([0,2]\text{.}\)
  • The interval \([0,1]\text{.}\)
  • The interval \([0,0.5]\text{.}\)
  • The interval \([1,2]\text{.}\)
  • The interval \([1,1.25]\text{.}\)
  • The interval \([1,1+h]\text{.}\)
  • The interval \([a,a+h]\text{.}\)
Solution

We can use either the Desmos calculator above, or algebra:

  • The average rate of change of \(f(x)\) over the interval \([0,2]\) is
    \begin{align*} \frac{f(2)-f(0)}{2-0}\amp = \frac{-5(2^2)+20(2)-(-5(0^2)+20(0))}{2}\\ \amp = \frac{20-0}{2}\\ \amp = 10. \end{align*}
    So the average rate of change in the height of the rocket between 0 and 2 seconds is 10 m/s.
  • The average rate of change of \(f(x)\) over the interval \([0,1]\) is
    \begin{align*} \frac{f(1)-f(0)}{2-0}\amp = \frac{-5(1^2)+20(1)-(-5(0^2)+20(0))}{1}\\ \amp = \frac{15-0}{1}\\ \amp = 15. \end{align*}
    So the average rate of change in the height of the rocket between 0 and 1 seconds is 15 m/s.
  • The average rate of change of \(f(x)\) over the interval \([0,0.5]\) is
    \begin{align*} \frac{f(0.5)-f(0)}{0.5-0}\amp = \frac{-5(0.5^2)+20(0.5)-(-5(0^2)+20(0))}{0.5}\\ \amp = \frac{8.75-0}{0.5}\\ \amp = 17.5. \end{align*}
    So the average rate of change in the height of the rocket between 0 and 0.5 seconds is 17.5 m/s.
  • The average rate of change of \(f(x)\) over the interval \([0,2]\) is
    \begin{align*} \frac{f(2)-f(1)}{2-1}\amp = \frac{-5(2^2)+20(2)-(-5(1^2)+20(1))}{1}\\ \amp = \frac{20-15}{1}\\ \amp = 5. \end{align*}
    So the average rate of change in the height of the rocket between 1 and 2 seconds is 5 m/s.
  • The average rate of change of \(f(x)\) over the interval \([1,1.25]\) is
    \begin{align*} \frac{f(1.25)-f(1)}{1.25-1}\amp = \frac{-5(1.25^2)+20(1.25)-(-5(1^2)+20(1))}{0.25}\\ \amp = \frac{17.1875-15}{0.25}\\ \amp = 8.75. \end{align*}
    So the average rate of change in the height of the rocket between 1 and 1.25 seconds is 8.75 m/s.
  • The average rate of change of \(f(x)\) over the interval \([1,1+h]\) is
    \begin{align*} \frac{f(1+h)-f(1)}{1+h-1}\amp = \frac{-5((1+h)^2)+20(1+h)-(-5(1^2)+20(1))}{h}\\ \amp = \frac{-5(h^2+2h+1)+20(1+h)-15}{h}\\ \amp = \frac{-5h^2-10h-5+20+20h-15}{h}\\ \amp = \frac{-5h^2+10h}{h}=\frac{(-5h+10)h}{h}\\ \amp = -5h+10. \end{align*}
    So the average rate of change in the height of the rocket between 1 and \(1+h\) seconds is \(-5h+10\) m/s.
  • The average rate of change of \(f(x)\) over the interval \([a,a+h]\) is
    \begin{align*} \frac{f(a+h)-f(a)}{a+h-a}\amp = \frac{-5((a+h)^2)+20(a+h)-(-5(a^2)+20(a))}{h}\\ \amp = \frac{-5(a^2+2ah+h^2)+20(a+h)-(-5a^2+20a)}{h}\\ \amp = \frac{-5a^2-10ah-5h^2+20a+20h+5a^2-20a}{h}\\ \amp = \frac{-10ah-5h^2+20h}{h}=\frac{(-10a-5h+20)h}{h}\\ \amp = -10a-5h+20. \end{align*}
    So the average rate of change in the height of the rocket between \(a\) and \(a+h\) seconds is \(-10a-5h+20\) m/s.
Checkpoint 7.2.3.

Consider a restaurant, whose weekly revenut, \(t\) weeks after opening is \(R(t)=60-40e^{-0.05t}\) thousand dollars. Find the average rates of change in weekly revenue over the following periods of time:

  • The interval \([1,3]\text{.}\)
  • The interval \([2,2.5]\text{.}\)
Solution

We can use either the Desmos calculator above, or algebra:

  • The average rate of change of \(R(t)\) over the interval \([1,3]\) is
    \begin{align*} \frac{R(3)-R(1)}{3-1}\amp = \frac{60-40e^{-0.05(3)}-(60-40e^{-0.05(1)})}{2}\\ \amp = \frac{-40e^{-0.05(3)}+40e^{-0.05(1)}}{2}\\ \amp\approx 1.81042896. \end{align*}
    So the average rate of change in the weekly revenue between 1 and 3 weeks is about \(\$1810.43\text{.}\)
  • The average rate of change of \(R(t)\) over the interval \([2,2.5]\) is
    \begin{align*} \frac{R(2.5)-R(2)}{2.5-2}\amp = \frac{60-40e^{-0.05(2.5)}-(60-40e^{-0.05(2)})}{0.5}\\ \amp = \frac{-40e^{-0.05(2.5)}+40e^{-0.05(2)}}{0.5}\\ \amp\approx 1.78724123611. \end{align*}
    So the average rate of change in the weekly revenue between 2 and 2.5 weeks is about \(\$1787.24\text{.}\)

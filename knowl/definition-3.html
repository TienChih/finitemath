Definition 6.2.1.

A polynomial \(p(x)\) is a function that can be written in the form

\begin{equation*} p(x)=a_nx^n+a_{n-1}x^{n-1}+\cdots+a_1x^1+a_0(x^0)\text{,} \end{equation*}

where \(n\geq 0\) is a whole number, and \(a_n\neq 0\) unless \(p(x)=0\text{.}\)

  • The \(a_i\) are the coefficients of \(p(x)\text{.}\)

  • \(a_n\) is the leading coefficient of \(p(x)\text{.}\)

  • \(n\) is the degree of \(p(x)\text{.}\)

  • \(a_nx^n\) is the leading term of \(p(x)\text{.}\)

Let \(A\) denote “produced in plant \(A\)” and \(I\) denote “passes inspection”. Note that we denote “produced in plant \(B\)” by \(P(A^c)\text{.}\) We are then given \(P(A)=0.6, P(I|A)=0.85, P(I|A^c)=0.7\text{.}\)

  1. We are finding \(P(A\cap I)=P(I|A)P(A)=(0.85)(0.6)=0.51\text{.}\) That is, 85% of the 60% of vehicles produced in \(A\) pass inspection, which is 51% of the vehicles produced in \(A\) and pass inspection.
  2. We are finding \(P(A^c\cap I)=P(I|A^c)P(A^c)=(0.7)(0.4)=0.28\text{.}\) That is, 70% of the 40% of vehicles produced in \(B\) pass inspection, which is 28% of the vehicles produced in \(B\) and pass inspection.
  3. Since all the vehicles are produced in \(A\) or \(B\text{,}\) if we take the 51% who pass inspection from \(A\) and the 28% who pass inspection from \(B\text{,}\) that is \(0.51+0.28=0.79\) or 79% of cars pass inspection in total. This let's us fill out the following:

Solution 8.3.17.1.

We note that there are 50 diners in all. Let \(C, F, T, S\) denote chicken, fish, steak and sick.

  1. We have that \(P(S)=\frac{5+4+3}{50}=0.24\text{.}\)
  2. Out of the 10 people who ate fish, 4 got sick, so \(P(S|F)=\frac{4}{10}\approx 0.4\text{.}\)
  3. We have that \(P(F\cup T)=\frac{4+6+3+12}{50}=0.5\text{.}\)
  4. We have that \(P(F\cup S)=\frac{4+6+5+3}{50}=0.36\text{.}\) We could also compute this \(P(F\cup S)=P(F)+P(S)-P(\cap S)=\frac{10}{50}+\frac{12}{50}-\frac{4}{50}=\frac{18}{50}=0.36.\)
  5. Out of the 12 people who got sick, 3 ate steak, so \(P(T|S)=\frac{3}{12}=0.25\text{.}\)
  6. We note that if you eat chicken, the chance you would get sick is \(P(S|C)=\frac{5}{25}=0.2\text{.}\) If you eat fish, the chance you would get sick is \(P(S|F)=\frac{4}{10}=0.4\text{.}\) If you eat steak, the chance you would get sick is \(P(S|T)=\frac{3}{15}=0.2\text{.}\) So Fish is the most likely to get you sick.

On the other hand, we know that for a binomial variable with \(n=100, p=0.5\) it has a mean or expected value of \(\mu=np=(100)(0.5)=50\) and standard deviation \(\sigma=\sqrt{np(1-p)}=\sqrt{100(0.5)(0.5)}=5\text{.}\) If we plot the normal curve with \(\mu=50, \sigma=5\) alongside the binomial distribution with \(n=100, p=0.5\text{,}\) we see that they match up remarkably closely!

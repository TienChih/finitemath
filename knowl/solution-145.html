Solution 9.3.4.1.

Let \(R\) denote “digits don't repeat” and \(E\) denote “digits are all even.”:

  1. Recall Example 9.3.2, we say \(P(R)=\frac{P(10,4)}{10^4}, P(E)=\frac{5^4}{10^4}, P(R\cap E)=\frac{P(5,4)}{10^4}\text{.}\) Thus
    \begin{equation*} P(R\cup E)= P(R)+P(E)-P(R\cap E)=\frac{P(10,4)+5^4-P(5,4)}{10^4}=\frac{5040+625-120}{10^4}=\frac{5545}{10000}=0.5545. \end{equation*}
  2. Note that \(P(R|E)=\frac{P(5,4)/10^4}{5^4/10^4}=\frac{P(5,4)}{5^4}=\frac{120}{625}=0.192\) .
  3. Since \(P(R|E)=0.192\neq0.504=P(R)\) so these are not independent.

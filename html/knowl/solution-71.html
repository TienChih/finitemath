Solution 6.2.4.1.

Let's let\(x\) denote the value by which we decrease the price of a widget from \(\$50\text{.}\) We'll note that the quantity sold is \(q=5x\text{.}\) From this we deduce:

  • The revenue is the price of a widget, times the number sold. So \(R(x)=(50-x)q=(50-x)5x\text{.}\)
  • The cost is the fixed cost of \(\$300\text{,}\) plus the marginal cost \(\$10\) times the number sold. So \(RCx)=300+10q=300+10(5x)=300+50x\text{.}\)
  • The profit is revenue minus cost. So \(P(x)=R(x)-C(x)=(50-x)5x-(300+50x)=-5x^2+200x-300\text{.}\)

Which by whatever method you most prefer, has vertex \((20,1700)\text{.}\) So the profit is maximized when the price is reduced by \(\$20\) or when the price is \(p=\$30\text{.}\) The profit here would be \(\$1700\) with \(20\cdot5=100\) widgets sold.

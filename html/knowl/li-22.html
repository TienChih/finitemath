Item 1.

We first identify the slope of the function:

\begin{equation*} m=\frac{\Delta C}{\Delta F}=\frac{100-0}{212-32}=\frac{100}{180}=\color{red}{\frac{5}{9}}\text{.} \end{equation*}

Then choosing either point, say \(\color{blue}{(32,0)}\text{,}\) and point slope form, we have:

\begin{align*} C-C_0\amp = m(F-F_0)\\ C-\color{blue}{0}\amp = \color{red}{\frac{5}{9}}(F-\color{blue}{32})\\ C\amp = \frac{5}{9}F-\frac{160}{9}\text{.} \end{align*}

Thus \(T(F)=\frac{5}{9}F-\frac{160}{9}\)
